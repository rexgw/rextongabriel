CNC Machining · Siemens NX CAM · GD&T Sep 2023 – May 2024
CAM MacBook Chassis Project
  • Developed a full CNC machining strategy for a MacBook chassis from solid aluminum billet (6061-T6) using Siemens NX CAM, programming a complete toolpath sequence spanning roughing, semi-finishing, and finishing operations across 15 individual operations; produced over 2,500 lines of G-code, carefully managing tool engagement, chip load, and spindle speed to achieve ±0.05mm dimensional tolerances and Ra surface finish requirements on all cosmetic and mating faces.
  • Designed a bespoke modular fixturing system consisting of 3 interchangeable clamp components, engineered to securely and repeatably locate the workpiece through a 4-setup machining sequence without exceeding deflection limits; the fixturing strategy reduced inter-setup changeover time by 40% and maintained workpiece deflection below 0.02mm during high-speed cutting passes, directly enabling the tight tolerances achieved on the final part.

SolidWorks · Thermoforming · Vacuum Infusion · DFM Sep 2023 – May 2024
RC Toy Car Modifications
  • Managed the full redesign workflow for an RC car platform, acting as lead engineer responsible for scoping modification requirements, assigning component redesign tasks, and integrating the new chassis, wheel, and body shell designs into a cohesive final assembly that improved performance characteristics and visual identity.
  • Designed a custom wheel geometry in SolidWorks optimized for 3-axis CNC machining, incorporating toolpath-friendly radii and draft angles to enable clean feature definition without specialized fixturing; separately designed a new body shell with geometry compatible with thermoforming, accounting for draft angles, wall thickness uniformity, and material draw behaviour.
  • Enhanced the car's modularity by engineering a system of 3D-printed peg attachment points across the chassis and body, enabling quick-swap customization of aerodynamic add-ons; designed the peg interface geometry for consistent fitment with minimal play, applying DFM principles in an additive manufacturing context.
  • Executed a 4-layer carbon fibre vacuum infusion layup on the redesigned chassis, carefully managing fibre orientation, resin flow front, and consolidation pressure throughout the infusion cycle; the resulting chassis demonstrated an 80% improvement in structural rigidity compared to the original injection-moulded component, validating the composite redesign approach.

SolidWorks · FEA · Composite Layup · Material Selection Oct 2023 – Present
Snowboard Design Project
  • Developed a parametric SolidWorks model of a snowboard with complex 3D geometry (camber and rocker) and performed FEA to evaluate core stress distribution under realistic rider loads.
  • Researched and selected optimal laminate materials (fiberglass, carbon fiber, resin, wood core) based on performance, manufacturability, and cost.
  • Fabricated a composite snowboard using a 3-layer fiberglass/carbon fiber wet layup with anisotropic properties to optimize torsional and flexural stiffness.
